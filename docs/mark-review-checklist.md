# Mark Review Checklist

Purpose: use this as the first review guide before spending more time polishing the Miller Custom Works draft. The goal is to get real answers, better photos, and a confirmed lead path.

## 1. Name and positioning

- Does `Miller Custom Works` feel like the right business/site name?
- Is `Custom home improvements built with third-generation craftsmanship` accurate and okay to feature?
- Does the site feel like Mark, or does any part sound too fancy, corporate, or generic?
- Should the site lean more toward custom woodwork, kitchens/built-ins, general home improvements, or a different specialty?

## 3. Contact and lead path

- What phone number should be public?
- Is text okay?
- What email should be public?
- Should the primary CTA be call, text, email, or a simple estimate form?
- What service area should the site claim?
- Are there areas or job types he does not want to take?
- Does he want people to send project photos before he talks with them?

## 4. Credibility wording

Do not publish these until confirmed.

- Is the business an LLC, sole prop, or other?
- Is he licensed in the relevant trade/location? If yes, what exact wording is allowed?
- Is he insured? Bonded? What exact wording is allowed?
- Are there associations, certifications, or years of experience that can be stated accurately?
- Can we use the legacy `A Third Generation Craftsman` wording from the old card?

## 5. Photo replacement list

For each project Mark wants to feature, ask for:

- 1 wide finished shot
- 1 medium angle
- 3 detail shots showing fit, finish, joinery, edges, hardware, trim, or texture
- before photos if available
- during/process photos if available
- project name or nickname
- location by town/area only, not full address
- what problem was solved
- materials or details worth mentioning

## 7. Copy and story prompts

Ask conversationally:

- How did you get into this work?
- Who taught you?
- What part of a job do you care about that most people do not notice right away?
- What does `done right` mean to you?
- What frustrates you about bad contractor work?
- What do you want people to feel when they walk into a finished room?
- Are patriotic or service-related details part of the real story, or should that stay subtle?

## 8. Decision after review

Only after this review, choose the next build move:

1. Replace placeholders and public-facing draft language.
2. Swap in better photos.
3. Confirm the contact path.
4. Expand the strongest project pages.
5. Decide whether this is ready for deployment.
